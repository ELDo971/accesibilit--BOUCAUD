Formula 1 2024 Grid

Red Bull

Red Bull enters 2024 with a bold, new car design inspired by Mercedes' unsuccessful concept. While the innovation has some questioning whether it will succeed where Mercedes failed, Max Verstappen's continued dominance is undeniable. However, Sergio Perez's position within the team could face challenges if he doesn't step up is game.

Mercedes

Mercedes faces a crucial year in 2024, navigating a period of flux amidst Lewis Hamilton's impending move to Ferrari in 2025. While their second-place finish in the 2023 constructors' championship masked underlying issues, their car lacked the dominant pace seen in previous years. This creates a two-pronged challenge for the team in 2024: firstly, refining their car design to regain championship-winning competitiveness, and secondly, identifying a suitable replacement for Hamilton who can seamlessly integrate and challenge for the top spot. With George Russell already confirmed in the other seat, the spotlight will be on the chosen driver as they strive to prove their worth alongside a proven talent like Russell. This season will be a test of Mercedes' adaptability and strategic thinking, as they aim to bridge the gap to Red Bull while simultaneously laying the groundwork for a successful future without Hamilton at the helm.

Ferrari

2024 promises to be a year of exciting change for Ferrari. Fred Vasseur's influence as team principal is expected to be evident in both the car and the team environment. Additionally, Lewis Hamilton's arrival in 2025 shakes things up considerably. This leaves Carlos Sainz with a crucial season to prove his worth and demonstrate he can compete at the highest level before vacating his seat for the legendary driver.The arrival of both Vasseur and Hamilton promises a new era of excitement and ambition for the iconic team.

Mclaren

McLaren enters the 2024 season with a wave of optimism. Buoyed by their impressive second half of 2023, which propelled them to a fourth-place finish in the constructors' championship, the team appears well-positioned to continue their upward trajectory. The long-term commitment of Lando Norris, who recently extended his contract, brings continuity and stability to the driver lineup, fostering an environment conducive to further development. Building upon these strengths, McLaren will aim to challenge the established frontrunners and potentially secure podium finishes more consistently. With the new regulations impacting the 2026 season on the horizon, maximizing their performance in 2024 will be crucial as they strive to solidify their position near the top of the midfield pack.

Aston Martin

Aston Martin's 2024 season is shrouded in a sense of "what could have been." Their initial surge in 2023, securing several podiums in the first half, raised hopes of a sustained challenge. However, the team's inability to maintain that momentum throughout the season raises questions about their consistency and development capabilities. While they finished a respectable fifth in the constructors' championship, their struggles in the latter stages highlight the need for significant improvement. Addressing these shortcomings and unlocking their early-season potential will be critical for Aston Martin if they wish to solidify their position as a top-five contender in 2024.

Alpine

Alpine faces an uphill battle in 2024. Dubbed the "French national team," their 2023 campaign fell short of expectations. Failing to match the advancements of McLaren and Aston Martin left them languishing in sixth place. The lack of significant progress throughout the season raises concerns about the team's development direction. Compounding their problems, the 2024 car reportedly suffers from excess weight, hindering potential performance gains. This suggests a challenging start to the season, leaving Alpine with the task of overcoming these hurdles and demonstrating a clear path towards improvement if they want to reclaim their position as a midfield leader.

Williams

Williams enters 2024 with cautious optimism after a season marked by incremental improvement. While still firmly in the back of the pack, they managed to close the gap to the midfield in 2023, occasionally threatening for points finishes. The question for 2024 is whether they can sustain this momentum and take the next step towards consistent points contention. Key to their success will be the continued development of their car and driver lineup, coupled with consistent performances throughout the season. If they can overcome the established pecking order, Williams might surprise and break into the top ten more regularly in 2024.

Visa Cashapp RB F1 team

The former AlphaTauri, now christened Visa CashApp RB, enters 2024 with a significant makeover. New leadership under team principal Laurent Mekies and a bold new sponsor partnership signal a fresh start for the Red Bull-affiliated team. While comparisons to Red Bull's "little sister" will likely persist, Visa CashApp RB aims to rewrite their narrative. With development resources more readily available under the Red Bull umbrella, and potentially a driver line-up bolstered by talent from the senior team, 2024 could be a year for them to emerge from the midfield pack and establish themselves as a legitimate force on the grid.

Stake F1 team Kick Sauber

2024 promises a year of transition for the rebranded Stake F1 Team Kick Sauber. While the "Kick Sauber" moniker serves as a temporary placeholder before Audi's arrival in 2026 (potentially causing sponsorship issues in some countries due to gambling restrictions), the focus will be on improvement. Despite the subjective nature of car design, the team will undoubtedly strive to refine their machine's performance, hoping to climb the grid and challenge for higher positions. With the resources of Stake and the expertise of Sauber's experienced personnel, the team has the potential to shake off their 2023 struggles and establish themselves as a more consistent contender in the midfield pack.
